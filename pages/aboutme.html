About me

Hello, I’m Douglas, a self-taught software engineer based in the SF Bay Area.

I started teaching myself to code about 5 years ago, purely out of curiousity.

I have a background in law and for several years have taught and tutored students as they prepare for the Law School Admissions Test (LSAT). The test involves careful reading of text coupled with strict logical thought.

I couldn't believe I hadn't found coding sooner, because it was merely an extension of the kind of thinking I'd been doing for years. But you also get to build things, which is way cooler and more fun that practicing law or teaching the LSAT.

I knew nothing but had plenty of resources. I took an online coding bootcamp and that gave me a good foundation in Ruby, Rails, SQL, JS, CSS, HTML, React, and Redux. projects.

I did stuff and sometimes it worked. Yeah, boi.

I don't know if I want to say I have a 'passion' for software engineering. I reserve that word for things like justice, positivity, and a deep musical groove. But I very much enjoy software engineering. But let's not go too nuts with the 'passion', eh?

Here are the technologies I use:

  • - HTML, CSS (Sass)
  • - JavaScript (ES6+)
  • - React
  • - Firebase, MongoDB
  • - Node.js
